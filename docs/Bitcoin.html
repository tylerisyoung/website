WHY BITCOIN: Bitcoin is going to the moon, end of story.

Some assets are better than others for certain applications, choosing to use a premium fuel vs a lower fuel grade; The benefits and tradeoffs of short term use case and consumer good type assets are seemingly clear; Save money at the pump vs Introducing a few additional cleaning solvents into your engine as you possibly should every few thousand miles in the hopes of it lasting longer. However when it comes to financial assets the tradeoff is less clear, should I buy silver or gold? (Hint: You should buy silver! ) & Why I chose Silver. They both have a physical existence, they are both easily accepted and converted back to cash, and they both have real world use cases which include industrial production, gold tooth crowns, use in solar cells, and much more, therefore the only way to determine their value, and thus their exchange rate with fiat dollars backed by central governments is to consider what buyers are willing to pay for such an asset. Investing Guidelines & Overview:

MY STRATEGY:

BUYING IN: Paying short term capital gains sucks, so lets avoid that as much as possible. Purchasing a crypto asset with USD from an ACH transfer from a bank is not a taxable event until realized, so it’s a good idea to buy and hold every asset for more than a year. If you choose to dollar cost average into the market (you should) during a bear market, it makes sense to first do this within a Roth IRA that allows you to hold crypto like itrustcapital.com. These purchases themselves will not be taxed, and they cann grow tax free until your retirement, so it’s probably a good idea to max that out as an individual with $6,000/yr. After that, it is likely best to continue cost averaging into the market either through an exchange like binannce.us (which I like), Blockfi.com (which I like), coinbase.com (use Coinbase Pro if at all), or crypto.com( which I use for a few alt coins unavailable elsewhere), or Voyager.com, celsius.network, and Gemini.com are also good options which I have not tried.

HODLING:

Market cycle holding: After purchasing your internet coins through ACH(cheaper) or debit card, you can leave them on these exchanges which is sometimes considered bad form from hacks which have taken place in the past. You can also transfer them to a more long term wallet like a “Ledger.” I prefer to keep my bluechip coins in Blockfi and earn aroud 5-8.6% interest on them depending on the coin.

Long Term Holdig: My long term hold currently is just my Roth IRA. The beauty of some of them (including itrust capital which I use) is that right on their website at any time, I can swap BTC-ETH-USD-GOLD-DOT-SILVER etc. all without paying any tax on that trade aside from a 1% fee. This is a perfect method to help dollar cost out of the market (into USD) without paying taxes on every trade. So when the bull market begins to near its peak (but hopefully it sees its traditional 60% corrections!) you can begin to pull your money out to buy the dips and dollar cost average back in once the reaccumulation phase begins again. Or you could simple leave everything in BTC/ETH/DOT/ADA and never worry about it until you retire, that works too.

As far as long term holding on my money in wallets and on exchanges, I try to hold the coins I buy for a year to avoid the ~40% capital gains tax rate here in CA, and I leave them in a place that earns me interest. If it happens that I would like to cost average out through a few weeks or months of what I consider to be a market cycle top, I will simply swap BTC etc. for USDC (not tether) which is tied to the US dollar, and leave that USDC ready to swap back to BTC etc. when I want to cost average back into the market. I like Blockfi for doing this, but as I understand, Voyager, Celsius, Gemini, etc. also offer similar things possibly with marginnally better rates.

CASHING OUT: Don’t do it! Just kidding, diversification of profits is key, especially when your portfolio has 10X’ed in a year or two. When you want to take profits from USDC -> USD or BTC -> USD just use whatever service you use to buy crypto via ACH and transfer it to your bank account. There may be some up front fees but it will work, and will be captured on the blockchain for tax purposes when you link your Binance, Blockfi, Coinbase etc. to a service like Cointracker.com for your taxes.

TAXES: This is generally for US Residents, and in refrence to myself, living in California. The IRS loves it’s taxes & your state has a local tax rate in addition to the federal tax rate of ___ because bitcoin and other currencies are considered “property,” and therefore are able to be taxed under capital gains during a taxable event.

Taxable events: Trading Crypto -> Fiat(USD) (Like transfering to your bank) Purchasing goods with crypto Trading one crypto for another (trading BTC for ETH or ETH for LTC)

Income Tax Events: Recieving Crypto as Income for work Staking Activities Mining Activities Airdrops Defi Lending Interest

Captured Losses of up to $3000 can be written off.
